Services

Personal Computers

Steve Jobs was instrumental in the development of Apple's line of personal computers, starting with the Apple II in the late 1970s and continuing with subsequent models such as the Macintosh, iMac, MacBook, and Mac Pro.

Operating Systems

Apple's Macintosh computers have been powered by various operating systems, including the Macintosh System Software, Mac OS, Mac OS X, and macOS, which were developed under Jobs' leadership.

Mobile Devices

Under Jobs' leadership, Apple introduced groundbreaking mobile devices, including the iPhone, iPad, and iPod Touch, which revolutionized the smartphone, tablet, and portable media player markets, respectively.

Software and Apps

Jobs oversaw the development of software applications and services that enhanced the user experience on Apple devices. This includes the iTunes media player and digital content store, iLife suite (iMovie, iPhoto, GarageBand, etc.), iWork productivity suite (Pages, Numbers, Keynote), and various apps available on the App Store.

Music and Media

Jobs was involved in the creation of services such as the iTunes Store, which allowed users to purchase and download music, movies, TV shows, books, and other digital content. He also played a key role in negotiations with major music labels, paving the way for legal music downloads through iTunes.

Online Services

Jobs was involved in the development and expansion of Apple's online services, including iCloud, a cloud storage and synchronization service, and the App Store, an online marketplace for third-party applications.
